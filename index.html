Introduction & Overview

üìñ 3 min read

India consolidated 29 legacy labor laws into four comprehensive Labour Codes, effective 21 November 2025. This is the most significant labor reform since independence. For HR, mastering these codes is essential for workforce transparency, equity, and trust.

The Code on Wages, 2019, merges four legacy acts. It directly affects salary structure, take-home pay, PF, overtime, gratuity, and minimum wages for every worker.

‚ö° 2-Day Full & Final Settlement
All wages payable on separation must be settled within two working days ‚Äî regardless of resignation, termination, or any other exit. Gratuity, leave encashment follow as soon as practicable. This demands real-time payroll reconciliation and automated F&F workflows.

Chapter 05

Contracts & Fixed-Term Employment

üìñ 3 min read

5.1 Mandatory Appointment Letters

‚ñæ

Every worker must get a written appointment letter within 3 months of rules taking effect. Must include position, wages, hours, terms.

5.2 Fixed-Term Employment Rules

‚ñæ
  • Same wages/benefits as permanent employees doing same work
  • ESI, EPF, bonus, gratuity proportionate to tenure
  • Gratuity after 1 year (service >6 months rounds up)
  • Auto-terminates on expiry if stated in advance
  • Cannot convert permanent roles to fixed-term to circumvent obligations

5.3 Contract Labour & Key Definitions

‚ñæ

Contract labour restricted for core activities. Separate grievance mechanisms for contract workers.

üìã Updated Definitions
Worker now includes sales promotion employees & working journalists. Supervisor threshold: ‚Çπ10K ‚Üí ‚Çπ18K/month.
Inter-state migrants: Round-trip home travel allowance every 12 months after 180 days.

5.4 Work-From-Home

‚ñæ

Formally recognized under Model Standing Orders for service-sector employment. HR should develop clear policies covering hours, data security, communication, and performance.

üìñ 5 min read

6.1 Termination & Notice

‚ñæ

1+ year service: one month's notice (or wages in lieu) + retrenchment compensationStatutory payment = 15 days' average pay √ó completed years of service. Paid when an employee is retrenched due to surplus labour, restructuring, or closure. = 15 days' average pay per completed year.

6.2 Retrenchment Threshold ‚Äî Major Change

‚ñæ
Previously

New Code

6.3 Strikes, Lock-outs & Unions

‚ñæ

14 days' notice for strikes (all establishments) and lock-outs. Prohibited during/7 days after conciliation. Union with 51%+ membership = sole negotiating union; otherwise, proportional negotiating council.

6.4 Grievance & Dispute Resolution

‚ñæ

Grievance committee mandatory for 20+ workers with women's representation.

  1. Conciliation ‚Äî by labor officers; formal prerequisite
  2. Arbitration ‚Äî mutual agreement
  3. Industrial Tribunals ‚Äî two-member panels

6.5 Domestic Inquiry Process

‚ñæ
  1. Show-cause notice with specific allegations
  2. Employee's written explanation
  3. Investigation with impartial inquiry officer
  4. Complete documentation of proceedings
  5. Action only if misconduct proven per standing orders
